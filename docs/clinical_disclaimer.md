# Clinical Disclaimer

**MAISI-RT Sandbox is not a medical device.**

This software and all outputs it produces — including QA reports, visualizations,
label maps, and case folders — are for **research, education, and AI prototyping
only**.

## What this project is NOT for

- Clinical diagnosis
- Treatment planning (radiotherapy or otherwise)
- Dose calculation or dose-volume histogram analysis
- Patient-specific quality assurance
- Clinical decision-making of any kind
- Regulatory submission or clinical trial data production

## Synthetic data limitations

- Synthetic CT and MR volumes are generated by AI models and may not reflect
  realistic patient anatomy.
- Segmentation masks are algorithmic outputs, not clinician-approved contours.
- CT Hounsfield Unit (HU) values from synthetic data have not been validated
  for downstream dosimetric use.
- MR signal intensities are synthetic and not calibrated to any scanner protocol.
- Label IDs and anatomical boundaries may be incorrect, incomplete, or
  inconsistent across model versions.

## Label map limitations

- Label maps provided in `configs/` are illustrative templates.
- Label IDs **must be verified** against the actual NVIDIA-generated mask
  definitions for the specific model version used.
- RT structure names in this project do not follow any specific clinical
  contouring guideline (e.g., RTOG, ESTRO, TG-263).

## Responsibility

Users of MAISI-RT Sandbox are solely responsible for:

- Verifying the appropriateness of any outputs before use in any downstream
  application.
- Complying with applicable regulations, institutional policies, and ethical
  guidelines.
- Ensuring that synthetic data is not mistakenly used as patient data.

## Contact and reporting

For questions about the framework, open an issue on GitHub.  
Do **not** submit patient-identifiable data in issues or pull requests.
